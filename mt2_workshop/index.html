Mutation Testing and Model Transformation Workshop

MT^2 was an interactive workshop on combining mutation testing and model transformation. It was held on 22nd July 2014 at the University of York in the United Kingdom, and organised by Louis Rose, Rob Alexander and John Clark. Its programme, informal proceedings, and summary are below.

Invited Speakers

Gordon Fraser is a lecturer in Computer Science at the University of Sheffield, UK. He received a PhD in computer science from Graz University of Technology, Austria, in 2007. The central theme of his research is improving software quality, and his recent research concerns the prevention, detection, and removal of defects in software. More specifically, he develops techniques to generate test cases automatically using search-based and constraint-based approaches, and to guide the tester in validating the output of tests by producing test oracles and specifications, for example using mutation analysis. Website: http://staffwww.dcs.shef.ac.uk/people/G.Fraser/

James Williams is a Research Associate in the Department of Computer Science at the University of York, UK, from where he also he received his PhD. His PhD research focused on devising an encoding for MDE models that enables metaheuristic search techniques to be applied to MDE problems. For instance, one could use the encoding to automatically search for an appropriate set of models to test a model transformation. James currently works on the OSSMETER EU STREP project, where he is responsible for developing a platform for applying metric analysis to open-source software projects, with the ultimate aim of assessing, comparing and improving their quality. Website: http://www.jamesrobertwilliams.co.uk
