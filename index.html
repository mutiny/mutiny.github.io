Mutiny
An experimental mutation testing framework for Ruby

About

Mutation testing asks "if your code is wrong, can your tests tell?" It's a simple but powerful idea that can find bugs in your code and improve your tests.

Mutiny is a modern implementation of mutation testing for Ruby. Mutiny is being developed as part of a research programme at the University of York.

Louis Rose

Louis leads the Mutiny project. He directs a research programme on mutation testing at the University of York, where he is a lecturer in software engineering.

He likes running (very slowly), learning (quite slowly) and finding bugs (quickly).

Tim Waterson

Tim is making it straightforward to apply Mutiny as part of a typical develop-test-build-deploy cycle.

Katherine Smith

Katherine is applying user-centered design and interaction modelling techniques to mutation testing, and delivering an improved user experience for Mutiny.

Liam Mullane

Liam is adding adding state-of-the-art equivalent mutant detection to Mutiny, which will improve its effectiveness, scalability and usability.

James Shaw

James is generalising the mutation engine in Mutiny so that it can be used for software written in more than one language.

A keen sportsman, he enjoys playing tennis and attempting to find his way around a golf course, albeit in a rather zig-zag fashion!

Katie Mills

Katie is analysing recently proposed techniques for speeding up mutation testing, and extending Mutiny to incorporate the most performant of these techniques.
